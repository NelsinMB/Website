My name is Nelsin Martin-Burnett. I am entering my fouth year at McGill University, in the Joint-Honours Mathematics and Computer Science program. In the Fall 2022 semester, I acted as a teaching assistant for an introductory computer architecture course, then was rehired for Winter 2023.

COURSES

Some highlights.

Logic and Computation

A followup to a course I found particularly challenging, Foundations of Programming Languages, this course opened my eyes to a whole new world, a world that I envision I have not spent my last of days in.

Honours Analysis 2

Probably my favorite major course at McGill. An introduction to a large amount of spaces. We covered a lot of material and often I find myself on Wikipedia trying to piece together the birds eye view of much of what we did. If I have any ability to write proofs today, I owe it in large part to this course.

Honours Algorithms & Data Structures

This course is a big boy, arguably one of the hardest courses I have taken at McGill. It is the canonical algorithms and data structures course, in that we did look at Cormen. Devroye is eccentric, the TAs are epic, and you'll be eclectic by the time you are done.

Introduction to Computer Architecture

This course gently pulled back the curtain that holds up much of the mystery behind 'the computer'. A course I have had the priviledge to be a teaching assistant for twice, I strongly recommend this course to anyone who hates magic.

American Literature 1

As an ardent hater of English for all 12 years of grade school, and quite a poor scholar in that regard, opting for this course served as a (partial) departure from childhood. I hope to formalize a tradition of trying things I hated in childhood. Up next: becoming proficient in French.

Organic Chemistry 1

Organic Chemistry 1 was not one of my favorite courses, but it showed me how lucky I am to find myself in the fields that I do. Up till this point, the thought of becoming a surgeon was burrowed somewhere in the depths of my conscious, and perhaps still is, but for the time being I'll stick where I am.
